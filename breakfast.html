Potato and Courgette Frittata

A savoury breakfast recipe of a traditional frittata, with a healthier twist containing courgette and feta cheese.

Potato and Courgette Frittata

Ingredients:

  • 1 small russet potato, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes,
  • 4 large eggs,
  • 2 egg whites,
  • 2 tablespoons coarsely chopped fresh coriander leaves,
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt,
  • 1/4 teaspoon hot sauce,
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil,
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped,
  • 1 small courgette, grated and squeezed dry in clean kitchen towel,
  • 90g queso fresco or feta cheese,
  • 2 strips cooked crumbled turkey bacon, optional.

Method:

1-Bring the potatoes with enough cold water to cover to a boil in a small saucepan. Cook, over medium-high heat, until the potatoes are tender, 6 to 8 minutes; drain and pat dry. Whisk together the eggs, egg whites, coriander, salt, and hot sauce in a large bowl.

2-Preheat the grill to medium-high. Heat the oil in a medium (10-inch) ovenproof nonstick pan over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and onion and cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent, 2 minutes.

3-Add courgette and cook until tender, about 6 minutes more. Add the cooked potatoes and cook, stirring occasionally, until the potatoes begin to brown slightly, about 4 minutes more.

4-Evenly pour the egg mixture over the vegetable mixture. Cook, over medium heat, tilting the pan and lifting the edges with a rubber spatula to let the uncooked egg flow underneath. Sprinkle with the cheese and the bacon, if using.

5-Broil, 5 to 7 inches from the heat, until the eggs puff and are just set and the cheese is golden brown, about 5 minutes. Remove carefully to a plate, or serve in wedges directly from the pan.


Courgette Hash Browns and Eggs

Easy to prepare and delicious harsh browns with an intense flavour of minced parsley and reasted red pepper.

Courgette Hash Browns and Eggs

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 2 teaspoons finely chopped fresh sage
  • 1 large clove garlic, minced
  • 1 small jalapeno pepper, minced with some seeds
  • 3 large courgettes or yellow summer squash, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 2 teaspoons white wine vinegar
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped roasted red pepper
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley or coriander
  • 8 large eggs

Method:

1-Heat the oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add the onions and cook, stirring, until soft and lightly browned, about 6 minutes. Add the sage, garlic and jalapenos and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds.

2-Add the courgettes, vinegar, 3/4 teaspoon salt and a few grinds of pepper and stir to combine. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until the squash begins to soften, 6 to 8 minutes. Uncover and add the red pepper and half of the parsley. Cook until the squash is completely soft and lightly brown, 6 to 8 minutes more. Remove from the heat.

3-Make 8 small indentations in the squash with a measuring cup or the back of a ladle. Crack the eggs into the indentations and sprinkle with salt and pepper. Return the pan to medium heat, cover and cook until the egg whites are set and the yolks are still runny, about 4 minutes.Sprinkle with the remaining parsley and serve.


Breakfast muffins

A very healthy sweet breakfast muffin with mixed seeds, bananas and blueberries. It's great to bake in advance, and refrigerate.

Breakfast muffins

Ingredients:

  • 2 large eggs
  • 150ml pot natural low-fat yogurt
  • 50ml rapeseed oil
  • 100g apple sauce or pureed apples (find with the baby food)
  • 1 ripe banana, mashed
  • 4 tbsp clear honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 200g wholemeal flour
  • 50g rolled oats, plus extra for sprinkling
  • 1½ tsp baking powder
  • 1½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1½ tsp cinnamon
  • 100g blueberry
  • 2 tbsp mixed seed (we used
  • pumpkin, sunflower and flaxseed)

Method:

1-Heat oven to 180C/160C fan/gas 4. Line a 12-hole muffin tin with 12 large muffin cases. In a jug, mix the eggs, yogurt, oil, apple sauce, banana, honey and vanilla. Tip the remaining ingredients, except the seeds, into a large bowl, add a pinch of salt and mix to combine.

2-Pour the wet ingredients into the dry and mix briefly until you have a smooth batter – don’t overmix as this will make the muffins heavy. Divide the batter between the cases. Sprinkle the muffins with the extra oats and the seeds.

3-Bake for 25-30 mins until golden and well risen, and a skewer inserted into the centre of a muffin comes out clean. Remove from the oven, transfer to a wire rack and leave to cool. Can be stored in a sealed container for up to 3 days.


Matcha breakfast bowl

A all in one yougurt and fruit bowl, best when in a hurry but you still want to have a healthy breakfast.

Matcha breakfast bowl

Ingredients:

  • 300g natural yogurt
  • 1 tbsp matcha powder
  • 2 tsp maple syrup
  • 1 kiwi fruit, sliced
  • 1 peach, sliced
  • 100g blueberries
  • 20g coconut flakes, toasted

Method:

Mix the yogurt with the matcha and maple syrup, and divide between two bowls. Top with the kiwi, peach and blueberries. Sprinkle over the coconut flakes just before serving.
